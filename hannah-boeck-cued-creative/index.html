The problem is that their website was built by a designer who has never sat across from a parent trying to figure out if their kid needs therapy. Most therapy websites are built to look good. But they describe services the way therapists think about them, not the way parents actually search for help. A parent doesn't Google "pediatric speech-language pathology services." They Google "my 3-year-old isn't talking" or "is it normal for a 5-year-old to stutter." After 1,000 builds, I can tell you the exact three pages that do 90% of the work. I can tell you the homepage mistake that's on 800 of those sites. And I can tell you what the practices getting 15+ inquiries a month are doing differently from the ones getting zero. Now I run a $500K business and I take three months off every year. Script 3: Idea-Led - "Your Skills Are Worth More Packaged"

"Here's something nobody tells you when you start a private practice. There's a ceiling. And most therapists hit it within two to three years."

You fill your schedule. You're seeing as many patients as you physically can. Revenue is good. But you can't grow it without either raising your rates or hiring another therapist and managing staff. Most practice owners I work with are already at that ceiling and they think the only options are work harder or hire people. But there's a third option most therapists never consider. Think about the last five parent consultations you did. How much of what you said was the same? The same explanations. The same home strategies. The same answers to the same questions. That repetition is a product. You just haven't packaged it yet. The screening protocol you've refined over the years. The intake process that saves you time. The parent education materials you created. The strategies you teach in every session. All of it has value beyond the one parent sitting in front of you. I know because I did it myself.
