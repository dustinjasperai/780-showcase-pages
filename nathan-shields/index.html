Script 1: The 3x Exit Hook  (45-60 sec)

"I sold my PT clinics for 3x the national average. Most practice owners get a fraction of that. Here's the difference."

When I sold my physical therapy clinics, the buyer didn't pay 3x because I had better patients or a better location. They paid it because the business ran without me. I hadn't treated a patient in years. The team ran operations. The KPIs were predictable. The systems were documented. That's what a buyer actually pays for. Most PT owners spend 10 or 15 years building a practice that's completely dependent on them. They're the lead therapist, the marketer, and the manager. Revenue drops the moment they step away. That's a practice worth 1x. Maybe less. Script 2: The "Get Out of Treatment" Hook  (35-50 sec)

"If you own a PT clinic and you're still treating 30 hours a week, you don't have a business. You have a job you can't quit."

I know because I was there. Patients asked for me by name. Referral sources sent to me specifically. I felt like if I stepped back, everything would fall apart. Then I figured out the system. I stepped out of treatment, built a team that ran the clinic without me, and eventually sold for 3x what most practice owners get. One of our coaching clients went from solo operator to a 9-person team that's more profitable than when they were treating full-time. Another doubled their facility size while cutting their own clinical hours in half. I'm hosting a free webinar that walks through exactly how to make this transition. How to step out of treatment without losing revenue, build a team, and turn your clinic into a real business. Register below. It's free and it's built specifically for PT practice owners who feel stuck.

Script 3: The Scaling Hook  (35-50 sec)

"Going from one clinic to two was the hardest thing I did. Going from two to three was easy. Let me explain."

The first expansion is brutal because you're building every system for the first time. Hiring when it's just you. Documenting when you've run on instinct for years. Learning to lead instead of treat. But once those systems exist, the second and third locations are just replication. Same hiring process. Same KPI dashboard. Same onboarding playbook. Most PT owners stay stuck at one location because they never build the systems that make the first expansion possible. They stay in treatment, stay overwhelmed, and hit a ceiling they can feel but can't break through. I'm running a free training on exactly this.
