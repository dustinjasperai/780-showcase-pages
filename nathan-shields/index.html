"I sold my four PT clinics for three times the national average. Most practice owners get a fraction of that. Here's the difference."

I hadn't treated a patient in years. My clinical director ran the floor. My billing manager ran the back office. The KPIs were predictable. The systems were documented. They're the lead therapist, the marketer, and the manager. Revenue drops the moment they step away. That's a practice worth one times. Maybe less. Script 2: The "Get Out of Treatment" Hook (45-55 sec)

"If you own a PT clinic and you're still treating thirty hours a week, you don't have a business. You have a job you can't quit."

I know because I was there. I opened my first clinic in 2002 and spent nearly a decade as the lead clinician, the marketer, and the manager. Patients asked for me by name. Referral sources sent to me specifically. I felt like if I stepped back, everything would fall apart. Then I hired a coach, joined a peer group, and made one decision that changed everything. I stopped trying to be a better clinician inside my business and I started trying to be the CEO of it. I stepped out of treatment, built a team that ran the clinic without me, and went on to sell four clinics at three times the national average. One of my coaching clients went from solo operator to a nine-person team that's more profitable than when she was treating full-time. Another doubled her facility size while cutting her own clinical hours in half. I'm hosting a free webinar called "The 3x Exit Playbook." Script 3: The Profit Paradox Hook (45-55 sec)

"Your clinic is busier than it's ever been. Your bank account is smaller than it was twelve months ago. This is the most common email I get from PT owners right now."

Full schedule. Therapists booked out. Patients waiting. And somehow margins are shrinking, payroll is climbing past sixty percent, and you're working harder for less. The problem is not patient volume. The problem is your business is leaking, and most owners can't see the leaks until someone walks them through the math. The four most common leaks I see across hundreds of PT clinics: payroll creep past sixty percent of revenue, provider utilization sitting at seventy when it should be eighty-five, plan-of-care completion at thirty percent when it should be sixty plus, and front-desk collections at fifty percent when they should be at a hundred. WebPT data alone says incomplete plans of care cost the average practice one hundred fifty thousand dollars a year. In my free webinar, I walk through the leak map, the KPIs every owner should run weekly, and the Three Pillars framework that turns a busy-but-broke clinic into a profitable, scalable, sellable asset.
