1

Set Up the Accounts

  • 5 bank accounts: Income, Profit, Owner's Pay, Tax, Operating Expenses
  • Physical account separation matters more than spreadsheet tracking
  • Contractors are visual and hands-on. Seeing the money in separate accounts changes behavior.
  • You stop "borrowing" from profit because it's no longer in the same pile
TALK: Why bank accounts, not spreadsheets. Contractors don't manage money on paper. They manage it by looking at a bank balance. If everything is in one account, you spend it. Separate the accounts and you physically cannot spend what's been allocated elsewhere.

2

Set Your Allocation Percentages

$1M Contractor

  • 5% Profit
  • 50% Owner's Pay
  • 15% Tax
  • 30% Operating Expenses

$3M Contractor

  • 10% Profit
  • 35% Owner's Pay
  • 15% Tax
  • 40% Operating Expenses

Target percentages shift as revenue grows. The system scales with you.

TALK: Walk through the numbers for their revenue level. At $1M, you're still heavily involved in the work, so owner's pay is higher. At $3M, you've hired more of the labor out, so operating expenses increase but profit percentage also increases.

3

Allocate Twice a Month

  • Every 1st and 15th, revenue gets allocated across all five accounts
  • Operating expenses has a ceiling, not a blank check
  • If you can't cover expenses, the system is telling you something: you're spending too much or pricing too low
  • No more guessing. The numbers surface the problem before it becomes a crisis.
TALK: The system tells you when something is wrong. Most contractors don't find out they have a spending problem until they're already in a hole. This system flags it every two weeks.

Take Your Profit Quarterly

  • Every quarter: take 50% of the profit account as a distribution
  • The reward that keeps the system motivating and real
  • Other 50% stays as a cash reserve for the business
  • You're building a war chest and paying yourself at the same time
TALK: Even if it's $2,000, the first distribution changes your relationship with the numbers. You've never taken profit on purpose before. That first check rewires how you think about every job you bid.
