Slide 7 of 25 / Your Differentiator

Plumbing is NOT HVAC!

Every other trades coach out there treats plumbing like it's the same as HVAC, electrical, or any other service business. It's not.

Plumbing has its own dynamics:

  • Different pricing structures than HVAC or electrical
  • Different service call patterns and emergency response
  • Different inventory challenges (right parts matter more than more parts)
  • Different customer expectations and sales processes
  • Different hiring and training needs for technicians

That's why you coach plumbers specifically. Not contractors in general. Everything in this webinar was built for plumbing.

The 4 Numbers That Tell You Everything

Track these daily. Know them by heart. They are the difference between a business and a job.

Section HeaderPart 2 intro
Slide 9 of 25 / Number One

Daily Job Count

"The Magic Number"

How many jobs did your shop run today?

This single number tells you two critical things:

01. Did you make money today?

If your daily job count drops below your breakeven threshold, you lost money that day. No exceptions.

02. Is it time to hire?

Consistently hitting capacity? You need another tech. Running light? Something is wrong with your marketing or phone handling.

If you don't know your daily job count right now, you're running your business blind.

The 4 NumbersYour core Magic Number concept
Slide 10 of 25 / Number Two

Close Rate

What percentage of the calls that come in turn into booked, completed, paid jobs?

Here's the benchmark most plumbers don't know:

88%

Once your shop exceeds an 88% close rate consistently, it's time to raise your prices.

Your market is telling you that you're underpriced.

If you're closing below 80%, the problem isn't your prices. It's your phone handling, your dispatch, or your techs. Either way, the close rate tells you exactly where to look.

The 4 NumbersYour 88% pricing benchmark
Slide 11 of 25 / Number Three

Average Ticket

What's the average dollar amount per completed service call?

If you are like most plumbers, you are grossly underpriced.

Most plumbing business owners set their prices based on what competitors charge, not on what the job actually costs them to deliver. They win jobs but lose money on half of them without even realizing it.

Your average ticket tells you:

  • Whether your pricing covers labor, overhead, materials, AND profit
  • Whether your techs are presenting options or just doing the minimum
  • Whether you're attracting the right kind of customer
  • Whether your price book needs updating (it should update quarterly)
The 4 NumbersPricing diagnostic
Slide 12 of 25 / Number Four

Truck Inventory ROI

You can't sell what you don't have.

Many plumbing companies send techs to repair calls with little or no inventory on the truck. The tech diagnoses the problem, drives to the supply house, comes back, finishes the job. Customer waits. Tech loses time. Revenue per call drops.

But here's what most owners get wrong about inventory:

$5,000

of the WRONG inventory

Parts the tech never uses. Old stock. Items for jobs that don't come up. Dead weight on the truck.

$800

of the RIGHT inventory

The 20-30 parts that cover 80% of service calls. Tech completes the repair on the first visit. 5x the repair revenue.

Right inventory beats more inventory every time.

Are You Charging What You're Worth?

Section HeaderPart 3 intro
Slide 16 of 25 / A Story Every Plumber Knows

The $200 Pipe

A homeowner calls a plumber. The plumber walks in, looks at the pipe, and hits it three times with a wrench. Problem fixed.

He hands the homeowner a bill for $200.

The homeowner says: "$200? You were here for five minutes! All you did was hit a pipe!"

The plumber says:

Let me itemize that for you:

Hitting the pipe: $5
Knowing where to hit it: $95
Knowing how hard to hit it: $100

You are not charging for your time. You are charging for your knowledge, your experience, and your ability to solve the problem right the first time. Price accordingly.

PricingThe classic plumber story for pricing
Slide 17 of 25 / The Pricing Truth

"If You Don't Ask, You Will Not Receive"

Most plumbing business owners are scared to raise their prices. Scared of losing customers. Scared of being more expensive than the guy down the street.

But here's the reality:

  • If your close rate is above 88%, your market is telling you to charge more
  • Your price should include labor, overhead, AND profit. Not just what competitors charge
  • Price books should update quarterly to stay current with material costs
  • Price changes need to be made thoughtfully, so you strengthen margins without damaging trust

The ones making excuses about why they can't raise prices? They're just scared and afraid to try.

PricingYour direct pricing truth
Slide 18 of 25 / A Warning

The Investment-Consolidation Robbery

Investment and consolidation companies have been acquiring profitable plumbing businesses across the U.S. You've probably heard about it. Maybe you've been approached.

Here's what they're looking for:

Sweet spot: $5 million in sales, better than $1 million in operating profit.

They offer large upfront prices with additional money spread over 3 years. Owners required to stay at least 3 years. "Bonus payouts" often fall short of what was promised.

The best defense is a business that's profitable enough to keep and systematized enough to sell on YOUR terms when YOU decide.
