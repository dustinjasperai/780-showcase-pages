Script 3: Idea-Led - One Fitting Set, One Time Slot (35-50 sec)

"Most ODs think adding specialty lenses means buying $50,000 in equipment and overhauling their schedule. It does not. It starts with one fitting set and one time slot per week."

The biggest misconception I hear from optometrists is that specialty contact lenses require a massive upfront investment and a complete practice restructuring. That belief keeps talented ODs on the sidelines while the keratoconus and post-surgical patients in their market go unserved or get referred out of town. Here is what it actually takes to start. One trial fitting set from a single manufacturer. Most cost a few hundred dollars. One dedicated time slot per week for specialty patients. Tuesday from 11 to noon. That is two hours out of your 40-hour week. You master that one lens system with that one weekly slot while your staff observes and learns the workflow. As demand grows and your team gets comfortable, you expand. But you do not start big. You start focused. I used this exact approach to build a practice of over 2,000 specialty patients with an average visit value exceeding $4,000.
