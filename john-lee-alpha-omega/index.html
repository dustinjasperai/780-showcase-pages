"I built a million-dollar chiropractic practice in Orange County, one of the most saturated chiropractic markets in the country. Most DCs there barely clear $300K. Here's what I did differently."

Script 2: The "Still Adjusting Every Patient" Hook  (35-50 sec)

"If you're the only doctor adjusting patients, answering phones between visits, and doing your own marketing at night, you don't have a practice. You have a treadmill."

I talk to chiropractors every week who are collecting $40K, $50K, even $60K a month and still exhausted. They can't take a vacation. They can't step away for a week without the schedule falling apart. Revenue is decent but the business can't function without them. That's not a scaling problem. That's a systems problem. And it's the most common ceiling in chiropractic. At my clinic in Irvine, I stepped out of day-to-day treatment years ago. The team runs the schedule, the new patient flow, and the operations. Collections stayed the same. Then they went up, because I finally had time to work on the business instead of just in it. Block scheduling, CA training protocols, and the leadership structure that holds it together. Register through the link below. It's built for practice owners collecting $25K or more who feel stuck at a ceiling they can't break through.

Script 3: The Associate Doctor Hook  (35-50 sec)

"Hiring an associate was supposed to grow my practice. Instead it nearly broke it. Here's what I learned the hard way so you don't have to."

Every chiropractor who's tried to bring on an associate knows the story. You hire someone with good skills, hand them patients, and hope for the best. Three months later they're underperforming, your patients are confused, and you're more stressed than before. The problem isn't the associate. It's the system you put them into. Or didn't. I've trained and managed multiple associate doctors at Lee Chiropractic in Irvine. I've also coached over 100 practice owners through the same transition. The ones who succeed all do the same three things before the associate ever starts: they document their clinical protocols, they build a training onboarding system, and they create clear KPIs so everyone knows what success looks like.
