The 7 Deadly Sins of Building a Contractor Business

  1. You ARE the Business
  2. No Financial Visibility
  3. Reactive Hiring
  4. No Documented Processes
  5. Underpricing
  6. No Marketing System
  7. No Accountability
The Contractors Coach
09
1

You ARE the Business

  • Every estimate, every call, every decision runs through you
  • If you disappear for two weeks, the business stops
  • You're not an owner. You're the most expensive employee.

The Fix: Document the 10 decisions you make most often. Train someone else to make them.

The Contractors Coach
10
2

No Financial Visibility

  • You know revenue but not margins by job type
  • Can't tell a banker or buyer what the business is worth
  • Financial decisions made by gut feel, not data

The Fix: Monthly P&L by service line. Not just annual tax prep.

The Contractors Coach
11
3

Reactive Hiring

  • Hire when desperate, not strategic
  • Warm bodies instead of team members
  • No onboarding, no clear expectations, high turnover

The Fix: Hire to a position description and a 90-day onboarding plan.

The Contractors Coach
12
4

No Documented Processes

  • How things get done lives in your head
  • Quality is inconsistent across crews and jobs
  • You can't delegate what isn't written down

The Fix: Document your top 10 repeating processes in simple step-by-step format.

The Contractors Coach
13
5

Underpricing

  • Price to win the job, not to make money
  • Know competitors' prices better than your own margins
  • Busy all the time but can't figure out where the profit went

The Fix: Cost-plus pricing with overhead and profit built in. Every job.

The Contractors Coach
14
6

No Marketing System

  • Depend entirely on referrals and word of mouth
  • When referrals slow down, revenue drops and you panic
  • No predictable way to generate leads you control

The Fix: Build at least one predictable lead generation channel you control.

The Contractors Coach
15
7

No Accountability

  • No measurable targets for anyone on the team
  • Problems get addressed as crises, never as trends
  • People do what they want because nobody tracks what they do

The Fix: Weekly team scorecards and 90-day action plans.
