The Hiring Problem Reframed

"I can't find good people." I hear this every single week. But the problem isn't the labor market. It's how you hire.

  • You hire reactively when you're drowning instead of proactively with a pipeline
  • You have no onboarding system, so the new guy quits in three weeks
  • You keep looking for a clone of yourself instead of someone trainable
  • Then you give up and go back to doing everything yourself

The Hiring Pipeline

  • Where to post and where you're wasting money posting
  • Screen for attitude over experience. You can teach pressure washing, you can't teach work ethic.
  • The onboarding checklist that gets a new crew member productive in week one, not month one
  • Always be hiring. The pipeline runs whether you need someone tomorrow or not.

Stop waiting until you're desperate. Build the pipeline before you need it.

2

Pricing Without the Owner

Most exterior cleaning owners underprice because they're guessing based on what competitors charge. Then they wonder why they're busy but broke.

  • Pricing by job type with real margin targets, not gut feel
  • Build pricing guides a crew leader or office admin can use
  • Stop approving every estimate yourself
  • Know your numbers so you can price for profit, not just to win the job

The Operational Playbook

  • Job pricing your crews can quote without calling you
  • A scheduling system that doesn't require your phone
  • A quality checklist that catches problems before the customer calls
  • Weekly reporting so you manage by numbers, not by being on site

Simple systems. Nothing fancy. Just documented processes that let the business run without your brain being the operating system.
