"Your opticians are technically skilled. They can adjust frames and read a prescription. But they've never been trained to help patients choose eyewear confidently, and it's costing you thousands every month."

Most optical staff learned on the job. They know the mechanics but not the conversation. When a patient hesitates on a second pair, they back off instead of presenting the value. When someone says "I'll think about it," they don't know what to say next. This isn't a staff problem. It's a training gap. Your team wants to do well. They just need the tools. I coach optical teams on frame styling, lens education, multiple-pair conversations, and objection handling. Not scripted sales pitches, but genuine confidence in guiding patients toward what they need. The practices I work with see the shift in weeks. Capture rates go up. Average ticket goes up.
