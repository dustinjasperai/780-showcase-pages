1

Gross Profit Margin by Service Line

  • Not your overall margin. Per service line. They are different.
  • The job you think makes the most money might be the one killing your margins
  • Healthy margins in trades typically range from 40-60%, depending on the service
  • If you don't know this number, you can't price correctly and you can't decide what to sell more of
ThinkTyler
08
2

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

  • What it actually costs to get a new paying customer through the door
  • Most owners have never calculated this because they've never tracked it
  • Ad spend + your time on estimates + referral costs + close rate = real CAC
  • If CAC is higher than first-job profit, you're paying to work
ThinkTyler
09
3

Breakeven Point

  • The monthly revenue number where you stop losing money
  • Below this number, every month costs you regardless of how busy you are
  • Most owners are surprised at how high it actually is once overhead is included
  • Changes how you think about monthly targets and seasonal planning
ThinkTyler
10
4

Cash Conversion Cycle

  • The time gap between spending money on a job and collecting payment for it
  • In trades, this gap is commonly 30 to 90 days
  • The number one reason profitable businesses run out of cash
  • Growth actually makes this worse if you don't manage it
ThinkTyler
11
5

Owner's Discretionary Earnings

  • The number a buyer looks at when valuing your business
  • Net profit + your salary + perks + one-time expenses = ODE
  • This is what determined the value of my engineering firm at exit
  • Most owners have never calculated it, even if they want to sell someday
ThinkTyler
12

How the 5 Connect

These aren't 5 separate things you track in isolation. Each one feeds the others.

  • Margin tells you which services are worth acquiring customers for
  • CAC tells you whether your marketing spend makes sense at those margins
  • Breakeven sets your monthly floor so you know the minimum target
  • Cash conversion determines whether profit on paper becomes cash in hand
  • ODE is the scoreboard that ties them all together into a single number

Miss one and the other four give you an incomplete picture.

15

Plumbing Company: CAC Was 3x What They Thought

A plumbing company was tracking their ad spend and assumed their customer acquisition cost was around $180. When they included the owner's time driving to estimates, the close rate on those estimates, and the admin time per lead, the real number was $540.

On their average job size, $540 CAC meant they were barely breaking even on first-time customers. Their entire profit came from repeat business they weren't systematically generating.

What Most Contractors Get Wrong

They hire an accountant to do their taxes and think that's financial management. It's not even close.

  • Tax prep is archaeology. It tells you what happened last year.
  • These 5 numbers tell you what's happening this week.
  • Your accountant files returns. They don't build dashboards or flag margin problems.
  • Financial management means using numbers to make decisions, not just to stay compliant

Compliance and strategy are two completely different functions. Most contractors only have one.
